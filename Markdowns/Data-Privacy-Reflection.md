### Best Practices for Handling Confidential Data
Best practices for handling confidential data include:
- Collecting only the minimum data required  
- Restricting access to authorised personnel only  
- Using secure, encrypted storage systems  
- Avoiding the sharing of sensitive data through unsecured channels  
- Following company policies and legal requirements for data handling  

These practices reduce the risk of unauthorised access or misuse.

---

### Responding to a Suspected Data Breach or Accidental Disclosure
If a data breach or accidental disclosure is suspected:
1. Report the incident immediately to the appropriate supervisor or security contact  
2. Avoid further sharing or modification of the affected data  
3. Document what happened and what data may be involved  
4. Follow company procedures for investigation and remediation  

Prompt reporting helps minimise harm and ensures compliance with legal obligations.

### Ensuring Secure Data Handling in Daily Tasks
To handle data securely in my daily tasks, I can:
- Use strong, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication  
- Lock my workstation when unattended  
- Keep software and systems up to date  
- Be cautious when accessing or handling sensitive information  

These steps help reduce the risk of accidental exposure or unauthorised access.

---

### Safe Storage, Sharing, and Disposal of Sensitive Information
- **Storage:** Store sensitive data only in approved, secure systems  
- **Sharing:** Share information strictly on a need-to-know basis using secure channels  
- **Disposal:** Delete data securely when it is no longer required, following retention policies  

Proper handling ensures confidentiality throughout the data lifecycle.

---

### Common Data Privacy Mistakes and How to Avoid Them
Common mistakes include:
- Using weak or reused passwords  
- Sending sensitive data via unsecured email or messaging apps  
- Storing confidential information on personal devices  
- Failing to log out of shared systems  

These issues can be avoided by following security policies, using secure tools, and maintaining awareness of data privacy responsibilities.
