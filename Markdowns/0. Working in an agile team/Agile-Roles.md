### Key Roles in an Agile Team

#### Developers
Developers are responsible for designing, building, testing, and maintaining software features. This includes front-end, back-end, and full-stack developers who collaborate to deliver working functionality that meets business and user requirements.

---

#### QA (Quality Assurance)
QA professionals ensure the quality of the product by testing features, identifying bugs, validating requirements, and confirming that software meets acceptance criteria before release.

---

#### Product Manager (PM)
The Product Manager defines the product vision and roadmap. They prioritise the backlog, gather requirements, communicate with stakeholders, and ensure the team is building features that deliver the most value to users and the business.

---

#### Agile Project Manager (Agile PM)
The Agile Project Manager facilitates Agile practices, removes blockers, supports team collaboration, and ensures that processes are followed effectively. They help the team stay organised and focused without micromanaging.

---

#### Other Supporting Roles
- **UX/UI Designers:** Create user-friendly and accessible designs.
- **Stakeholders:** Provide business input and feedback.
- **Customer Support:** Share user feedback and real-world issues to improve the product.

---

### Collaboration in Kanban vs. Scrum

**Scrum:**
- Work is delivered in fixed-length sprints.
- Roles collaborate through structured ceremonies such as sprint planning, stand-ups, reviews, and retrospectives.
- Developers, QA, and PMs work closely within sprint boundaries.

**Kanban:**
- Work flows continuously without fixed sprints.
- Collaboration focuses on maintaining flow, limiting work in progress (WIP), and responding to priorities in real time.
- Roles interact more asynchronously, updating tasks as they move across the board.

Both frameworks encourage transparency and collaboration, but Kanban allows more flexibility while Scrum provides more structure.
